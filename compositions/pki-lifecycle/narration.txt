PKI. The certificate lifecycle, in seven steps.

One: key generation. The entity creates a keypair.

Two: CSR submission. The request is sent to the certificate authority.

Three: validation. The CA verifies the requesting identity.

Four: issuance. The CA signs and returns the certificate.

Five: deployment. The cert is installed on the server.

Six: active use. Every TLS handshake relies on this.

Seven: renewal. Before expiry, the loop begins again.

And when something goes wrong, revocation. CRLs and OCSP cut compromised certs from the trust chain.

From keypair to renewal.
